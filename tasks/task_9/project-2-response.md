---

### 1. Escapism (Philippe-bn)

The first project that caught my eye was ‘Escapism’ by Philippe-bn. It is very similar to my own project, which was great to see. 

* This student did the added bonus of having a camera controller and allowing the player to move around, as well as having dynamic elements with the flamingoes. 
* I tried refreshing the page a few times and noticed that the terrain does not refresh every time like mine does, but that’s alright! 
* I appreciate how their terrain looks a lot more ethereal than mine does. I value art that looks beautiful, as it is something that I struggle with myself. It was cool to see a similar project to mine that excelled in a lot of aspects compared to mine.

---

### 2. Click the Cube (lee-sifu)

The next project I looked at was ‘Click the Cube’ by lee-sifu. I really appreciate how this student was comfortable incorporating silliness and spontaneity in their project. 

* I am someone who finds it difficult to “let loose” and be okay with things that don't always match. This student did a great job of exploring different backgrounds and stickers. 
* I love that the program responds to you spam clicking the cube. It’s natural for a player to want to do that after a few clicks, and it got a chuckle out of me when I saw that there is a safeguard against clicking too much.

---

### 3. Final Project (aydanmcgrail)

The last project I looked at was by aydanmcgrail. I sometimes marvel at the ideas that some people have in my CART classes. 

* I never would have thought of making a cute little fighting game myself for this project. 
* I’m going to be honest; I didn’t end up winning even after several attempts. I would have appreciated a way to block or something similar.
* The audio added to the silliness of the game, along with the creepy photos in the background. I don't think this is meant to be taken seriously as a fighting game, which makes it enjoyable to play and just be okay with being entertained. 

---

Overall, reviewing these projects was a great reminder of how diverse the creative process can be. I am continuously astounded at how creative people can be. Seeing other peoples’ work is a great reminder to keep my mind open and to continue exploring and interacting with the world.
